The target audience for this website is men and women between the ages of 15 and 35. These are people who want to be aware of the weather in their area so they can plan the apropriate activites with friends and family.

  • Persona 1: Matt is 30 and works as an engineer but loves spending time outside with his family when he gets home from work. He likes to be aware of what the weather will be like so he doesn't plan a swimming activity when it's going to rain. Ya know...
  • Persona 2: Natalie is 17 and in her senior year of High School. She is involved in a lot of extra-curricular activities, most of which take place outside. In addition to those, she loves hanging out with her friends and teammates when they don't have to be somewhere for school purposes. She loves knowing what's going on so she and her friends can plan exciting adventures.

Scenario 1: Matt is finishing up work and wants to take his kids to the park to play soccer. He looks up the weatherandmore.co website to see what it says the weather will be like in his city. He finds out that it is supposed to be a little breezy and cloudy with a slight change of rain. He doesn't want to pressure his kids into going to the park when they'll probably be cold, so he decides to bring up the option of a movie night with his wife when he gets home.

Scenario 2: Natalie is in her seventh period class and is already thinking about plans with her friends after school since she doesn't have practice today. She thinks about going with friends to the farmer's market, but wants to check the weather first. She pulls up the website and finds out that it's going to be sunny and pretty warm. On her way to her last class, she tracks down her friends and asks their opinion. They think it's a great idea, escpecially since there is a splash pad just across the street that they can go to if it gets too hot.

Scenario 3: Matt is hanging out with some of his college friends that are in town and he brings up the option of going to the drive-in theater that night. His friends seem a bit skeptical, so he pulls up the website on his phone to check what the weather will be like. He tells them that it will be clear skies and sixty-five degrees, perfect weather to watch the movie, star gaze a bit, and all they'll need is a light jacket to be comfortable. His friends are persuaded and they start making preparations.

Scenario 4: Natalie is packing her soccer bag so she will be ready for their game the following day. She goes to her clost and notices her lightweight althetic jacket hanging up. She decides to check the weather to see if she'll need that or not. She pulls up the website on her phone and notices that it is supposed to be cloudy and slightly breezy. She packs the jacket because it's better to have it and not need it, than need it and not have it.

Scenario 5: Matt and his wife are talking about plans for the following week since it is summer and the kids don't have school. They talk about going rock climbing and swimming at the outdoor rec area, but decide to double check that the weather will be good. They assume it will because it's summer, but in their area, they have had some surprising weather changes in the past. They pull up the website and see that it is supposed to be in the high 70's. They smile at each other and make the plans with the kids.
