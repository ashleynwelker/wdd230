Eagle Mountain, Utah

Brief History

Eagle Mountain is a young town that has grown at a breakneck pace. While the area was along the pony express trail in the 1860s, the town only had a population of 250 when it was incorporated in 1996. Since then it has grown rapidly and become the largest city by land space in Utah County, and the population has jumped from 250 to over 35,000 in a little over 20 years. It has also produced two Olympians. The rapid city growth has been stressful on city leadership, with the town having thirteen different mayors in twenty-one years, with only one making it to the end of her term. With its rapid growth, Eagle Mountain is also a young town, with almost half the population under age eighteen.

Demographic information

In 2019, Eagle Mountain, UT had a population of 32.4k people with a median age of 20 and a median household income of $83,290. Between 2018 and 2019 the population of Eagle Mountain, UT grew from 29,919 to 32,412, a 8.33% increase and its median household income grew from $77,935 to $83,290, a 6.87% increase. The 5 largest ethnic groups in Eagle Mountain, UT are White (Non-Hispanic) (83.8%), White (Hispanic) (8.36%), Two+ (Non-Hispanic) (3.27%), Other (Hispanic) (2.18%), and Asian (Non-Hispanic) (0.679%). 0% of the households in Eagle Mountain, UT speak a non-English language at home as their primary language. 96.5% of the residents in Eagle Mountain, UT are U.S. citizens. In 2019, the median property value in Eagle Mountain, UT was $280,700, and the homeownership rate was 87.5%. Most people in Eagle Mountain, UT drove alone to work, and the average commute time was 31.4 minutes. The average car ownership in Eagle Mountain, UT was 2 cars per household.
